# Acme Industrial Quality Standard (V2)

## Chapter 1: General Provisions

### 1.1 Scope and Applicability

#### 1.1.1 Definition of scope

The scope of the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS) encompasses all manufacturing processes and related activities conducted within Acme facilities. This standard shall apply to all personnel engaged in production, quality assurance, and management functions. Compliance with the AIQS is mandatory for all operations to ensure consistent quality and adherence to regulatory requirements. Any deviations from this standard shall be documented and reviewed by the quality management team.

#### 1.1.2 Applicability to personnel

The AIQS shall be applicable to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors involved in the manufacturing process. Training programs shall be established to ensure that all relevant personnel understand their responsibilities under this standard. Records of training completion shall be maintained and made available for review. Non-compliance by any personnel shall result in corrective actions as deemed necessary by management.

#### 1.1.3 Exclusions from the standard

Certain activities may be excluded from the scope of the AIQS, including those related to research and development, unless specified otherwise. Any exclusions shall be documented in accordance with clause 5.1.1 and justified by the quality management team. It is the responsibility of the team to ensure that all relevant stakeholders are informed of these exclusions. Regular reviews shall be conducted to assess the necessity of maintaining such exclusions.

#### 1.1.4 Review and updates

The AIQS shall be reviewed annually to ensure its continued relevance and effectiveness, including alignment with clause 3.2.4. Updates may be initiated based on changes in regulatory requirements, industry best practices, or operational needs. Stakeholder feedback shall be solicited during the review process to identify areas for improvement. All revisions shall be documented, and the updated standard shall be communicated to all personnel affected by the changes.

### 1.2 Normative References

#### 1.2.1 General applicability of references

Normative references shall be applicable to the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS) and shall provide essential guidelines for compliance. These references shall include industry standards, regulatory documents, and best practices that are recognized within the manufacturing sector. The applicability of each reference shall be determined based on its relevance to specific processes and products. Compliance with these references, including requirements specified in clause 5.1.3, shall be monitored to ensure alignment with the AIQS requirements.

#### 1.2.2 Updating normative references

Normative references shall be reviewed and updated periodically to reflect changes in industry standards and practices. A schedule for the review process shall be established, and any amendments to the references shall be documented. Stakeholders shall be informed of any changes to ensure that all parties are operating under the most current guidelines. The responsibility for maintaining the accuracy of normative references shall rest with designated quality assurance personnel.

#### 1.2.3 Access to normative references

Access to normative references shall be ensured for relevant personnel involved in the implementation of the AIQS. These references shall be made available in both physical and digital formats to facilitate ease of use. Training shall be provided to relevant staff on how to interpret and apply these references effectively. A centralized repository shall be established to house all normative documents, ensuring that they are readily accessible to authorized users.

#### 1.2.4 Documentation of normative references

Documentation of normative references shall be maintained to provide a clear record of all applicable standards and guidelines. Each reference shall be cataloged with relevant details, including title, version, and date of publication. This documentation shall be reviewed periodically to ensure its completeness and accuracy. Records shall be retained for a minimum of five years to support audits and compliance assessments related to the AIQS.

#### 1.2.5 Training requirements for LOTO

All personnel involved in maintenance activities shall receive training on the Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures as part of their onboarding process. The Operational Risk Reduction (ORR) program shall include periodic refresher training to ensure that employees remain knowledgeable about current safety practices. Records of training completion shall be maintained for auditing purposes. Non-compliance with training requirements may lead to restricted access to maintenance tasks.

### 1.3 Definitions and Abbreviations

#### 1.3.1 Definitions of LOTO and NCR

The terms 'LOTO' and 'NCR' shall be defined as follows: 'LOTO' refers to the Lockout/Tagout procedure utilized to ensure that machinery is properly shut off and not able to be started up again prior to the completion of maintenance or servicing. 'NCR' denotes a Non-Conformance Report, which is a formal document generated when a product or process fails to meet specified requirements. Both terms are essential for maintaining safety and quality standards within manufacturing operations.

#### 1.3.2 Definitions of LOTO and ORR

'LOTO' and 'ORR' shall be defined as follows: 'LOTO' is the Lockout/Tagout procedure that safeguards personnel during maintenance activities by preventing the unexpected energization of machinery. 'ORR' stands for Operational Readiness Review, which is a systematic evaluation conducted to ensure that Class-B rotating equipment and processes are prepared for safe and effective operation. Understanding these definitions is critical for compliance with safety and operational protocols.

#### 1.3.3 FAI and LOTO definitions

'FAI' and 'LOTO' shall be defined in the context of quality assurance as follows: 'FAI' refers to First Article Inspection, a process that verifies that the first production unit meets all specified requirements before full-scale manufacturing begins.

#### 1.3.4 FAI and LOTO definitions (continued)

'LOTO' is the Lockout/Tagout procedure that is implemented to protect workers during the FAI process by ensuring that machinery is in a safe state. These definitions are vital for maintaining quality and safety standards.

#### 1.3.5 FAI and ORR definitions

'FAI' and 'ORR' shall be defined as follows: 'FAI' stands for First Article Inspection, which is conducted to confirm that the initial production run meets the required specifications. 'ORR' refers to Operational Readiness Review, a critical assessment performed to ensure that all systems are functional and compliant prior to operational deployment. These definitions are essential for ensuring product quality and operational efficiency.

#### 1.3.6 Supplier Change Notification

Changes to approved suppliers for critical consumables shall be notified in writing at least 45 days before effect. A containment action may be imposed if the receiving process is unable to validate the new source within the notification window.

### 1.4 Roles and Responsibilities

#### 1.4.1 Operator responsibilities for LOTO

Operators shall be responsible for the implementation of the Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures as outlined in the Operational Risk Reduction (ORR) documentation. Each operator is required to ensure that all energy sources are effectively controlled during maintenance activities. Compliance with LOTO procedures shall be monitored regularly to ensure adherence to safety standards. Failure to follow these procedures shall result in disciplinary actions as specified in the company policy.

#### 1.4.2 FAI process and NCR handling

The First Article Inspection (FAI) process shall be conducted to verify that manufacturing processes meet specified requirements. Any non-conformance identified during the FAI shall be documented as a Non-Conformance Report (NCR).

#### 1.4.3 FAI process and NCR handling (continued)

The NCR shall be reviewed by the quality assurance team, and corrective actions shall be implemented in a timely manner. Documentation of the FAI and NCR findings shall be retained for future reference and compliance audits.

#### 1.4.4 FAI and LOTO integration

The integration of the First Article Inspection (FAI) process with Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures shall be established to ensure that all equipment is properly secured during initial production runs. Prior to conducting an FAI, operators shall verify that LOTO procedures are followed to mitigate risks associated with energy release. Documentation of both the FAI and LOTO compliance shall be maintained to support quality assurance efforts and regulatory requirements.

### 1.5 Documentation Requirements

#### 1.5.1 First article inspection documentation and general documentation practices

Documentation related to the First Article Inspection (FAI) shall be completed and submitted prior to the commencement of production. The FAI documentation shall include all necessary measurements, test results, and compliance certifications as specified in the relevant quality standards. This documentation shall be reviewed and approved by designated quality assurance personnel to ensure adherence to established requirements. Records of the FAI shall be retained for a minimum of five years from the date of approval. Additionally, all documentation related to manufacturing processes shall be maintained in a clear and organized manner. Each document shall be version-controlled and dated to ensure traceability. Operators are required to follow established procedures for document creation, revision, and distribution. Documentation shall be accessible to all relevant personnel to facilitate compliance with quality standards and operational efficiency.

#### 1.5.3 FAI report retention

The FAI report shall be retained as part of the quality documentation for each production run. This report shall serve as a reference for future inspections and audits. Any discrepancies identified during the FAI process shall be documented and addressed in accordance with established corrective action procedures. Retention of the FAI report shall comply with the organization's record retention policy.

#### 1.5.4 NCR documentation requirements

In the event of a Non-Conformance Report (NCR) arising from the FAI process, detailed documentation shall be generated to outline the nature of the non-conformance and the corrective actions taken. The NCR documentation shall include references to the FAI findings and any subsequent investigations. All NCR records shall be reviewed and approved by quality assurance personnel, and retained for a period of no less than three years to ensure compliance and facilitate continuous improvement.

## Chapter 2: Equipment Qualification

### 2.1 Asset Identification and Tagging

#### 2.1.1 Equipment tagging requirements

All equipment utilized within the manufacturing process shall be assigned a unique identification tag. Tags shall cover all asset classes, including servo-hydraulic presses and injection molding units, to ensure accurate tracking and maintenance. Tags shall be affixed in a manner that is clearly visible and resistant to wear, thereby facilitating easy identification by personnel. Regular audits shall be conducted to verify the presence and legibility of these tags.

#### 2.1.2 Documentation of asset details

A comprehensive inventory of all tagged equipment shall be maintained, including details such as the asset number, location, and operational status. Equipment such as precision surface grinders shall be included in this inventory, which shall be updated promptly following any changes in equipment status or location. This documentation shall be retained for a minimum of five years to support traceability and compliance with quality standards.

#### 2.1.3 Tagging for maintenance records

Maintenance records for each piece of equipment shall reference the corresponding identification tags. Equipment classified as 5-axis CNC machining centers and vertical turning lathes shall have their maintenance activities logged in accordance with established procedures. These records shall include dates of service, nature of maintenance performed, and personnel responsible. Such documentation is essential for tracking equipment performance and ensuring compliance with maintenance schedules.

#### 2.1.4 Asset identification verification

Periodic verification of asset identification shall be conducted to ensure that all equipment is accurately tagged and documented. The verification process shall include checks for the presence of tags on equipment such as 5-axis CNC machining centers and vertical turning lathes. Any discrepancies identified during this process shall be addressed immediately, and corrective actions shall be documented to maintain the integrity of the asset identification system.

#### 2.1.5 Documentation of ORR results

All results from the Operational Readiness Review shall be documented in a formal report. This report shall include details of the assessment process, findings, and any corrective actions required. Records of the ORR shall be maintained in accordance with the company’s documentation retention policy. Access to these records shall be granted to authorized personnel for review and audit purposes.

#### 2.1.6 Decommissioning procedures

Decommissioning of equipment shall be conducted in accordance with established procedures. Injection molding units shall be evaluated for any residual hazards prior to decommissioning. All necessary documentation shall be completed to ensure compliance with safety regulations. A designated team shall oversee the decommissioning process to maintain adherence to these procedures.

### 2.2 Installation Qualification

#### 2.2.1 Installation procedures documentation and verification of installation accuracy

Documentation of installation procedures shall be maintained for all equipment. This documentation shall include step-by-step instructions, diagrams, and specifications necessary for proper installation. Records shall be reviewed and approved by qualified personnel prior to implementation. Any deviations from the documented procedures shall be recorded and justified to ensure compliance with the established standards. Additionally, verification of installation accuracy shall be conducted upon completion of the installation process. This verification shall include checks against the manufacturer's specifications and the installation procedures documented. Any discrepancies identified during verification shall be addressed promptly, and corrective actions shall be documented. A report summarizing the verification results shall be generated and retained for future reference.

#### 2.2.3 Training of installation personnel

Personnel involved in the installation of equipment shall receive appropriate training to ensure competency in installation practices. Training programs shall cover the specific requirements of the equipment being installed and the relevant installation procedures. Records of training completion shall be maintained for each individual, and refresher training should be conducted periodically to ensure ongoing compliance with installation standards.

#### 2.2.4 Post-installation review process

A post-installation review process shall be implemented to assess the effectiveness of the installation. This review shall involve a comprehensive evaluation of the installation outcomes against the predefined criteria. Feedback from installation personnel and stakeholders shall be collected to identify areas for improvement. Any findings from the review shall be documented, and necessary adjustments to the installation procedures shall be made to enhance future installations.

### 2.3 Operational Readiness Review

#### 2.3.1 Conducting the ORR

An Operational Readiness Review (ORR) shall be conducted prior to the initiation of any new operational phase. The ORR shall evaluate the readiness of equipment, personnel, and processes to ensure compliance with established operational standards. Documentation of the ORR findings shall be prepared and retained for future reference. The review shall involve all relevant stakeholders to ensure comprehensive assessment and approval.

#### 2.3.2 Equipment assessment requirements

Hydraulic presses shall undergo a thorough assessment during the ORR process. This assessment shall include verification of operational functionality, safety compliance, and maintenance status.

#### 2.3.3 Equipment assessment requirements (continued)

Any deficiencies identified during the review shall be documented and addressed prior to the equipment being deemed operationally ready. Approval for use shall be contingent upon satisfactory completion of these assessments.

#### 2.3.4 Follow-up actions post-ORR

Following the completion of the ORR, any equipment identified as requiring further action shall be addressed promptly. For 5-axis CNC machining centers, the results of the ORR shall dictate necessary modifications or repairs. A follow-up review shall be scheduled to confirm that all corrective actions have been implemented effectively. Documentation of these actions shall be appended to the original ORR report for comprehensive tracking.

### 2.4 Periodic Requalification

#### 2.4.1 Requalification frequency

Periodic requalification of equipment shall occur at defined intervals to ensure continued compliance with operational standards. The frequency of requalification shall be determined based on equipment usage, criticality, and historical performance data. A schedule shall be established and maintained to document the timing of each requalification event. Any deviations from the established schedule shall be justified and documented accordingly.

#### 2.4.2 Equipment performance evaluation

Prior to requalification, a comprehensive performance evaluation shall be conducted for Class-B rotating equipment such as vertical turning lathes and coordinate measuring machines. This evaluation shall include an assessment of operational parameters, calibration status, and any maintenance records. Results from the evaluation shall be documented and reviewed to determine if the equipment meets the necessary specifications for continued use. Non-conformance shall trigger corrective actions as required.

#### 2.4.3 Documentation of requalification

All requalification activities shall be thoroughly documented to maintain an accurate record of compliance. Documentation shall include the date of requalification, personnel involved, results of performance evaluations, and any corrective actions taken. Records pertaining to precision surface grinders shall be retained for a minimum of five years. These records shall be accessible for review by authorized personnel.

#### 2.4.4 Corrective actions for non-conformance

In the event that equipment such as precision surface grinders or coordinate measuring machines fails to meet requalification criteria, immediate corrective actions shall be initiated. A root cause analysis shall be performed to identify underlying issues, and a corrective action plan shall be developed and implemented. Follow-up evaluations shall be conducted to verify the effectiveness of the corrective actions taken. Documentation of these processes shall be maintained as part of the requalification records.

### 2.5 Decommissioning

#### 2.5.1 Equipment removal process

The removal of equipment, such as injection molding units, shall be performed by qualified personnel. Prior to removal, all operational connections shall be safely disconnected, and the equipment shall be rendered inoperable. A checklist shall be utilized to verify that all steps have been completed. This process shall ensure that the equipment is safely transported to the designated decommissioning area.

#### 2.5.2 Lockout/tagout requirements

Prior to the decommissioning of any equipment, including injection molding units, a Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedure shall be implemented. This procedure shall ensure that all energy sources are effectively isolated and that the equipment cannot be inadvertently re-energized during the decommissioning process. Documentation of the LOTO procedure shall be maintained as part of the decommissioning records.

#### 2.5.3 Record retention

Records related to the decommissioning process shall be retained for a minimum of five years. These records shall include documentation of procedures followed, personnel involved, and any incidents that occurred during the decommissioning. Retention of these records shall facilitate future audits and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.

#### 2.5.4 Data Integrity Requirements

Records generated by automated inspection equipment shall be stored in tamper-evident form with an audit trail showing user, timestamp, and any value modifications. Manual overrides of inspection results shall be traceable to a qualified technician and accompanied by a documented justification.

## Chapter 3: Operational Controls

### 3.1 Pre-operation Checks

#### 3.1.1 General pre-operation requirements

Prior to commencing any operational activities, a comprehensive pre-operation check shall be conducted. This check shall include an assessment of all relevant equipment, ensuring that each component is functioning correctly and safely. Operators shall verify that all safety devices are in place and operational. Documentation of the pre-operation check shall be maintained for future reference and compliance verification.

#### 3.1.2 Operator training verification

Prior to conducting pre-operation checks, operators shall be required to demonstrate competency in the use of the Class-B rotating equipment. This verification shall include a review of the operator’s training records and practical assessments. Only those operators who have successfully completed the necessary training shall be permitted to perform pre-operation checks. Documentation of operator training shall be retained to ensure compliance with training standards.

#### 3.1.3 Reporting and addressing issues

Any issues identified during the pre-operation checks shall be reported immediately to the designated supervisor. A formal process shall be established for documenting these issues, including the nature of the problem and the corrective actions taken. Operators are required to refrain from operating any Class-B rotating equipment until all reported issues have been resolved and the equipment has been deemed safe for operation.

### 3.2 Torque and Fastener Controls

#### 3.2.1 Calibration of torque equipment

Torque measurement equipment associated with servo-hydraulic presses shall be calibrated at specified intervals to ensure accuracy. Calibration records shall be maintained and made available for review. The calibration process shall follow the manufacturer's specifications and relevant industry standards. Any deviations identified during calibration shall be addressed promptly to maintain compliance with operational controls.

#### 3.2.2 Torque application procedures

Operators shall adhere to established torque application procedures to ensure proper fastening of components. These procedures shall include the use of appropriate torque settings based on the specifications provided for each assembly. Training shall be provided to all personnel involved in the fastening process to ensure understanding and compliance with these procedures.

#### 3.2.3 Inspection of fasteners

Fasteners shall be inspected prior to installation to verify that they meet specified quality standards. Any fasteners that exhibit signs of damage or wear shall be removed from service and replaced. Inspection records shall be maintained to document the condition of fasteners and ensure traceability throughout the manufacturing process.

#### 3.2.4 Documentation of parameters

All process parameters shall be documented in a manner that allows for traceability and review. Documentation should include the date, time, and personnel responsible for monitoring. Records shall be retained for a minimum of three years to facilitate audits and process evaluations. It is recommended that electronic systems be utilized to enhance data accuracy and accessibility.

#### 3.2.5 Equipment-specific inspections

Each piece of equipment shall undergo a specific inspection as outlined in the operational manual. Operators are required to follow the established checklist for each equipment type, including the verification of critical parameters such as fluid levels, pressure settings, and electrical connections. Any discrepancies identified during this inspection shall be addressed prior to operation, and records of these inspections shall be retained.

#### 3.2.6 Adjustment of parameters

Adjustments to process parameters shall be performed only by qualified personnel following established procedures. Any changes made to the parameters must be documented, including the rationale for the adjustment and the expected impact on the process. A review of the adjusted parameters shall be conducted to ensure they remain within acceptable limits. It is advisable to implement a verification step post-adjustment to confirm the effectiveness of the changes.

### 3.3 Tooling and Fixture Controls

#### 3.3.1 Tooling inventory management

All tooling and fixtures, including those for vertical turning lathes, shall be recorded in a centralized inventory management system. This system shall include details such as location, usage history, and maintenance schedules. Regular audits of the tooling inventory shall be conducted to ensure accuracy and compliance with operational requirements. Any discrepancies identified during audits shall be documented and addressed promptly to maintain operational integrity.

#### 3.3.2 Tooling maintenance procedures

Routine maintenance procedures for all tooling and fixtures shall be established and documented. These procedures should include inspection intervals, maintenance tasks, and responsible personnel. Operators are encouraged to report any signs of wear or damage to ensure timely maintenance actions. Adherence to these maintenance procedures shall be monitored to ensure consistent performance and reliability of the tooling.

#### 3.3.3 Tooling calibration requirements

Calibration of tooling and fixtures, including those for vertical turning lathes, shall be performed at specified intervals as defined in the calibration schedule. Calibration records shall be maintained to demonstrate compliance with quality standards. Any tooling found to be out of calibration shall be removed from service until recalibrated. The calibration process shall be conducted by qualified personnel to ensure accuracy and reliability of measurements.

#### 3.3.4 Tooling disposal protocols

Disposal protocols for tooling and fixtures shall be established to ensure compliance with environmental regulations. All tooling that is no longer serviceable shall be evaluated for disposal methods that minimize environmental impact. Documentation of disposal actions shall be maintained for accountability. Personnel involved in the disposal process shall be trained on applicable regulations and procedures to ensure proper handling.

### 3.4 Process Parameter Controls

#### 3.4.1 Monitoring process parameters

Process parameters shall be continuously monitored to ensure compliance with established specifications. The monitoring system shall include equipment associated with 5-axis CNC machining centers, which is designed to provide real-time data on critical parameters. Any deviations from the specified limits shall be recorded and addressed promptly to maintain operational integrity. Regular calibration of monitoring equipment shall be conducted to ensure accuracy and reliability of the data collected.

#### 3.4.2 Validation of control systems

Control systems utilized for managing process parameters shall undergo validation to confirm their effectiveness and reliability. This validation process shall include equipment such as injection molding units, which play a critical role in ensuring that the control mechanisms function as intended. Validation activities shall be documented, and any identified deficiencies shall be addressed before the systems are put into operation. Regular revalidation shall be scheduled to maintain compliance with operational standards.

### 3.5 Energy Isolation and LOTO

#### 3.5.1 Lockout/tagout procedures

Lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures shall be implemented to ensure that equipment, including servo-hydraulic presses, is properly isolated from energy sources during maintenance or servicing activities. Authorized personnel are required to follow the established LOTO procedures, which include the application of locks and tags to energy-isolating devices. These procedures shall be documented and made accessible to all relevant personnel to promote compliance and safety during operations.

#### 3.5.2 Verification of energy isolation

Prior to commencing any maintenance work, personnel shall verify that the energy isolation for equipment, such as injection molding units, has been effectively implemented. This verification process shall include testing of the equipment controls to ensure that no energy is present. Documentation of this verification shall be maintained to provide a record of compliance with energy isolation protocols and to ensure the safety of all personnel involved.

#### 3.5.3 Training requirements

Personnel involved in energy isolation and lockout/tagout procedures shall receive training to ensure understanding of the associated risks and the proper application of LOTO practices. This training shall cover the identification of energy sources, the use of locks and tags, and the procedures for verifying energy isolation. Regular refresher training sessions should be conducted to maintain awareness and compliance with safety standards.

#### 3.5.4 Periodic audits and reviews

Periodic audits of lockout/tagout procedures shall be conducted to assess compliance and effectiveness. These audits shall include a review of documentation, training records, and adherence to established protocols. Any deficiencies identified during the audits shall be addressed promptly, and corrective actions shall be documented to ensure continuous improvement in energy isolation practices.

## Chapter 4: Inspection and Measurement

### 4.1 First Article Inspection

#### 4.1.1 First article inspection process

The first article inspection process shall be conducted prior to the commencement of production runs. This process involves a comprehensive evaluation of the initial production unit against established specifications. All measurements and observations shall be documented to ensure traceability. Any discrepancies identified during this inspection shall be addressed and resolved before proceeding with further production activities.

#### 4.1.2 Documentation requirements

Records of the first article inspection shall be maintained for a minimum of five years. These records shall include detailed reports of measurements, inspection results, and any corrective actions taken. Documentation shall be readily accessible for review by authorized personnel. Failure to maintain these records shall result in non-compliance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard.

#### 4.1.3 Personnel qualifications

Personnel conducting first article inspections shall possess the necessary qualifications and training to perform the required evaluations. Operators are required to demonstrate proficiency in measurement techniques and the use of inspection tools. Regular training sessions should be scheduled to ensure that all personnel remain current with inspection protocols and standards. Non-compliance with personnel qualifications may lead to invalid inspection results.

#### 4.1.4 Corrective action procedures

In the event that a first article inspection reveals non-conformance with specified requirements, a corrective action procedure shall be initiated. This procedure shall include a root cause analysis and the implementation of corrective measures to prevent recurrence. All corrective actions taken shall be documented and reviewed for effectiveness. Continuous improvement practices should be applied to enhance the inspection process.

### 4.2 In-process Inspection

#### 4.2.1 Inspection frequency requirements

In-process inspections shall be conducted at predetermined intervals to ensure compliance with quality standards. The inspection frequency shall be based on the criticality of the process and the historical performance data. For processes utilizing precision surface grinders, inspections shall occur at regular intervals. Any deviations from the established frequency shall be documented and justified to maintain quality assurance.

#### 4.2.2 Documentation of inspections

Records of in-process inspections shall be maintained for all operations involving precision surface grinders. These records shall include the date and time of inspection, the identity of the inspector, and the results of the inspection. Documentation shall be retained for a minimum of three years and shall be made available for review during internal audits or external assessments to ensure compliance with quality standards.

#### 4.2.3 Use of measurement tools

Measurement tools employed during in-process inspections shall be calibrated and verified for accuracy prior to use. For coordinate measuring machines, specific measurement tools shall be utilized to ensure that all dimensions and tolerances meet specified requirements. Calibration records shall be maintained and shall include the date of calibration, the calibration method used, and the individual responsible for the calibration process.

#### 4.2.4 Corrective actions for nonconformance

In the event that nonconformance is detected during in-process inspections, immediate corrective actions shall be initiated. For processes involving coordinate measuring machines, the affected production batch shall be quarantined until a thorough investigation is conducted. The findings shall be documented, and corrective actions shall be implemented to prevent recurrence. Follow-up inspections shall be performed to verify the effectiveness of the corrective measures taken.

#### 4.2.5 Environmental Impact Assessment

New Class-B rotating equipment introductions shall include an environmental impact statement covering energy consumption, coolant and lubricant waste streams, and noise exposure. The statement shall be reviewed by the facility sustainability lead prior to installation qualification.

### 4.3 Final Inspection and Release

#### 4.3.1 Final inspection procedures

Final inspection procedures shall be established to ensure that all products meet specified quality criteria prior to release. These procedures shall include a comprehensive review of dimensional, visual, and functional characteristics as defined in the product specifications. Inspection records shall be documented and maintained for traceability. Any deviations from the established criteria shall be addressed before the product is released for distribution.

#### 4.3.2 Non-conformance reporting

In the event that a product does not meet the specified quality criteria, a Non-Conformance Report (NCR) shall be generated. The NCR shall document the nature of the non-conformance, the affected product details, and any corrective actions taken. All NCRs shall be reviewed by the quality assurance team to determine the appropriate disposition of the non-conforming product. Records of NCRs shall be retained for future reference and analysis.

#### 4.3.3 NCR resolution process

The resolution process for Non-Conformance Reports (NCRs) shall be clearly defined and communicated to all relevant personnel. Corrective actions shall be implemented in a timely manner to address the issues identified in the NCR. Follow-up inspections shall be conducted to verify the effectiveness of the corrective actions taken. Documentation of the resolution process shall be maintained to ensure compliance with quality standards.

#### 4.3.4 First article inspection requirements

A First Article Inspection (FAI) shall be conducted for new or modified products prior to full-scale production. The FAI shall verify that the manufacturing process is capable of producing products that meet all specified requirements. All findings from the FAI shall be documented, and any discrepancies shall be resolved before proceeding with production. Records of the FAI shall be retained to support future audits and quality assessments.

### 4.4 Measurement System Analysis

#### 4.4.1 General measurement system requirements and calibration procedures

Measurement systems shall be evaluated to ensure their suitability for the intended application. This evaluation shall include an assessment of accuracy, precision, and stability. Operators are required to document the results of these evaluations and maintain records for future reference. The measurement system shall be regularly reviewed to confirm ongoing compliance with established standards and specifications. Additionally, calibration procedures shall be established for all measurement instruments utilized within the manufacturing process. These procedures should specify the frequency of calibration, the standards to be used, and the personnel authorized to perform the calibrations. Calibration records shall be retained for a minimum of five years to ensure traceability and compliance with regulatory requirements.

#### 4.4.3 Measurement system verification

Verification of measurement systems shall be conducted at regular intervals to ensure their accuracy and reliability. Coordinate measuring machines shall undergo a specific verification process that includes comparison against known standards. Any discrepancies identified during verification shall be documented, and corrective actions shall be implemented promptly to maintain measurement integrity.

#### 4.4.4 Data analysis and reporting

Data generated from measurement systems shall be analyzed to identify trends and variations that may impact product quality. Reports summarizing this analysis shall be generated and reviewed by quality assurance personnel. Recommendations for process improvements based on data analysis should be communicated to relevant stakeholders to facilitate continuous improvement efforts.

### 4.5 Calibration Management

#### 4.5.1 Calibration schedule requirements

A calibration schedule shall be established to ensure that all measuring and test equipment is calibrated at specified intervals. This schedule shall include the identification of equipment, the calibration frequency, and the responsible personnel. The calibration schedule shall be reviewed annually to determine its effectiveness and to incorporate any necessary adjustments based on equipment performance and usage patterns.

#### 4.5.2 Calibration documentation protocols

Documentation of calibration activities shall be maintained for all measuring and test equipment. Records shall include the date of calibration, the individual performing the calibration, the results of the calibration, and any adjustments made. These records shall be retained for a minimum of five years and shall be readily accessible for review by authorized personnel.

#### 4.5.3 Out-of-tolerance procedures

In the event that measuring or test equipment is found to be out of tolerance during calibration, a procedure shall be followed to address the nonconformance. This procedure shall include the identification of affected measurements, the investigation of potential causes, and the implementation of corrective actions. Documentation of these actions shall be maintained to ensure compliance with quality management requirements.

## Chapter 5: Nonconformance and Corrective Action

### 5.1 Detection and Reporting

#### 5.1.1 Reporting procedures

All personnel shall be trained to recognize and report instances of nonconformance. Reports shall be submitted to the designated quality control department using the established reporting format. Timely reporting is essential to facilitate immediate investigation and corrective action. Records of all reported nonconformances shall be maintained for review and analysis.

#### 5.1.2 Follow-up actions

Upon detection of nonconformance, follow-up actions shall be initiated to assess the impact on product quality. Investigations shall be conducted to determine the root cause, and corrective actions shall be implemented as necessary. Documentation of these follow-up actions shall be retained for future reference and to support continuous improvement efforts within the quality management system.

#### 5.1.3 Digital Work Instruction Rollout

Work Instructions distributed through the digital authoring system shall include a hash-based version identifier visible on every printed page. Offline copies older than 90 days shall not be used for production operations without verification against the current digital version.

### 5.2 Containment

#### 5.2.1 Nonconformance reporting process

In the event of a nonconformance, a Nonconformance Report (NCR) shall be generated to document the issue. The NCR shall include details of the incident, including the equipment involved, the nature of the nonconformance, and any immediate containment actions taken. Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures shall be followed to ensure that affected equipment is rendered inoperable during the investigation process. The NCR shall be reviewed by the quality assurance team to determine the appropriate corrective actions.

#### 5.2.2 Implementation of LOTO procedures

Prior to any containment actions, Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures shall be implemented to ensure the safety of personnel and equipment. All operators are required to verify that LOTO measures are in place before addressing any nonconformance issues. Documentation of the LOTO process shall be maintained and made available for review during audits. Failure to adhere to LOTO procedures may result in disciplinary actions as outlined in the safety policy.

#### 5.2.3 Containment measures

Containment measures shall be established to prevent the spread of nonconforming products or processes. These measures may include physical barriers, isolation of affected areas, and the use of designated containment containers. Personnel involved in the containment process shall be trained in the specific procedures required to manage nonconformance effectively. Regular assessments of containment effectiveness shall be conducted to ensure compliance with established protocols.

#### 5.2.4 Monitoring and review

The effectiveness of containment actions shall be monitored continuously until the nonconformance is resolved. A review of containment measures shall be conducted at regular intervals to evaluate their adequacy and to identify any necessary improvements. Records of monitoring activities shall be maintained for future reference and analysis. Continuous improvement initiatives shall be implemented based on findings from the review process.

### 5.3 Root Cause Analysis

#### 5.3.1 Initiation of analysis

Root cause analysis shall be initiated upon the identification of any nonconformance. The responsible personnel shall document the nonconformance details, including the date, time, and nature of the issue. A preliminary assessment shall be conducted to determine the potential impact on product quality and compliance. This documentation shall serve as the foundation for further investigation and corrective action planning.

#### 5.3.2 Data collection requirements

Data collection shall be performed systematically to support the root cause analysis process. Relevant records, including production logs, inspection reports, and any related documentation, shall be gathered and reviewed. Personnel involved in the process shall ensure that all pertinent information is captured accurately to facilitate a comprehensive understanding of the nonconformance. This data shall be retained for future reference and analysis.

#### 5.3.3 Analysis techniques

Various analysis techniques shall be employed to identify the root cause of the nonconformance. Techniques such as the 5 Whys, Fishbone Diagram, or Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) shall be utilized as appropriate. The selected method shall be documented, and the rationale for its use shall be provided. This approach shall ensure a thorough examination of contributing factors and promote effective corrective actions.

#### 5.3.4 Preventive action procedures

Preventive actions shall be established to mitigate the risk of potential nonconformances. These procedures should include regular reviews of processes, training programs for personnel, and maintenance schedules for equipment. The effectiveness of preventive actions shall be evaluated periodically, and adjustments shall be made as necessary to enhance overall quality management. Documentation of preventive actions shall be maintained to support continuous improvement initiatives.

### 5.4 Corrective and Preventive Action

#### 5.4.1 Nonconformance reporting

Nonconformances shall be documented through the issuance of a Nonconformance Report (NCR). Each NCR shall include a detailed description of the nonconformance, the date of occurrence, and the personnel involved. The responsible department shall ensure that all NCRs are reviewed and acknowledged by relevant stakeholders. Records of NCRs shall be maintained for a minimum of three years to facilitate trend analysis and continuous improvement efforts.

#### 5.4.2 Root cause analysis

A root cause analysis shall be conducted for each identified nonconformance to determine the underlying factors contributing to the issue. This analysis shall involve a systematic examination of processes, equipment, and personnel actions. The findings from the root cause analysis shall be documented and communicated to relevant parties to ensure understanding and prevent recurrence of the nonconformance.

#### 5.4.3 Implementation of corrective actions

Corrective actions shall be developed and implemented in response to identified nonconformances. These actions should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART). Responsibility for the implementation of corrective actions shall be assigned to designated personnel, and progress shall be monitored to ensure effectiveness. Documentation of the corrective actions taken shall be retained for future reference.

### 5.5 Records Retention

#### 5.5.1 FAI records retention

Records related to First Article Inspection (FAI) shall be retained for a minimum period of five years from the date of approval. These records shall include all documentation that verifies compliance with specified requirements and any associated corrective actions taken. Access to FAI records shall be controlled to ensure that only authorized personnel may review or modify the documentation. Upon expiration of the retention period, records shall be disposed of in a manner that protects sensitive information.

#### 5.5.2 General records management

All records pertaining to nonconformance and corrective action processes shall be systematically organized and maintained. These records should be stored in a secure location to prevent unauthorized access and loss of information. Regular audits of the records management system should be conducted to ensure compliance with established retention policies. Personnel responsible for record-keeping shall receive training on the importance of accurate documentation and retention practices.

#### 5.5.3 NCR documentation retention

Nonconformance Report (NCR) documentation shall be retained for a minimum of three years following the resolution of the nonconformance. This documentation shall include details of the nonconformance, corrective actions taken, and verification of effectiveness. Records shall be reviewed periodically to ensure compliance with retention requirements and to facilitate continuous improvement efforts. Upon reaching the retention limit, NCR records shall be securely destroyed to maintain confidentiality.

#### 5.5.4 Retention policy review

The records retention policy shall be reviewed annually to ensure its effectiveness and compliance with applicable regulations. Any necessary updates to the policy shall be documented and communicated to all relevant personnel. Changes to retention periods or procedures shall be based on operational needs and regulatory requirements. Stakeholders shall be involved in the review process to provide input and ensure that the policy meets the needs of the organization.

#### 5.5.5 Cybersecurity Controls

Equipment connected to the site network shall be registered in the IT asset inventory prior to operational release. Access credentials shall be issued per the site identity management procedure and reviewed quarterly. Firmware updates shall be applied within 30 days of vendor release unless an approved deferral is recorded.

#### 5.5.6 Reporting and follow-up

The findings from the root cause analysis shall be compiled into a report that includes identified causes, contributing factors, and recommended corrective actions. This report shall be distributed to relevant stakeholders for review and approval. Follow-up actions shall be tracked to ensure implementation of corrective measures, and effectiveness shall be evaluated to prevent recurrence of the nonconformance. Records of these actions shall be maintained for audit purposes.
