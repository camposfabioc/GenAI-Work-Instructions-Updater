# WI-024: Surface Grinder Wheel Dressing Procedure

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Work Instruction (WI) is to outline the procedure for dressing the grinding wheel on the Precision Surface Grinder (Asset ID: 2847.310.0055). Proper dressing ensures optimal wheel performance, enhances surface finish quality, and maintains compliance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS) requirements, specifically per AIQS 2.4.3.

## 2. Scope
This WI applies to all operators and maintenance personnel involved in the operation and upkeep of the Precision Surface Grinder. It encompasses the steps necessary to prepare, execute, and verify the wheel dressing process, ensuring adherence to AIQS standards, particularly AIQS 3.4.3.

## 3. Prerequisites
Before commencing the wheel dressing procedure, personnel shall complete the following prerequisites: 
1. Conduct an Operational Readiness Review (ORR) of the Precision Surface Grinder (2847.310.0055) to confirm it is in safe working condition, as outlined in AIQS 3.1.2.
2. Review the machine's operating manual and safety guidelines.
3. Ensure all required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) is worn, including safety glasses, gloves, and hearing protection.
4. Complete Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedures to ensure the machine is de-energized and cannot be inadvertently started during the dressing process, following AIQS 2.2.2.
5. Prepare the necessary tools and materials, including a diamond dressing tool and cleaning supplies, ensuring compliance with AIQS 2.2.4.

## 4. Procedure
1. **Preparation**: Ensure the Precision Surface Grinder is powered off and has been properly locked out per LOTO procedures. Gather the diamond dressing tool and cleaning materials.
2. **Inspection**: Inspect the grinding wheel for any visible damage or wear. Document any findings in a Nonconformance Report (NCR) if necessary, as per AIQS 3.4.3.
3. **Dressing the Wheel**:
   - Adjust the diamond dressing tool to the appropriate height and angle for the grinding wheel.
   - Engage the dressing tool slowly against the wheel surface while the grinder is running at a low speed.
   - Move the dressing tool across the wheel in a consistent pattern to ensure even dressing. Repeat this process until the wheel surface is true and free of any irregularities.
4. **Cleaning**: After dressing, clean the wheel and surrounding area to remove any debris or particles generated during the process. Use a soft brush or vacuum cleaner to ensure a clean workspace.
5. **Final Inspection**: Conduct a final inspection of the dressed wheel to ensure it meets quality standards. Document the completion of the dressing process and any observations in the machine log.

## 5. Verification
Upon completion of the wheel dressing procedure, the operator shall verify that the grinding wheel is in optimal condition and free from defects. This verification shall include a visual inspection and a performance test during the initial grinding operation. Any discrepancies shall be reported and documented according to AIQS 3.1.2 and AIQS 2.4.3. Ensure that all records of the dressing process are maintained for future reference and compliance audits.
