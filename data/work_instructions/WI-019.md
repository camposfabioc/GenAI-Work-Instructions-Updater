# WI-019: Visual Inspection Criteria for Surface Defects

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Work Instruction (WI) is to establish the criteria for conducting visual inspections of surface defects on manufactured components. Adherence to these criteria ensures compliance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS) and maintains product integrity, enhancing customer satisfaction per AIQS 4.1.4.

## 2. Scope
This WI applies to all personnel involved in the visual inspection of components produced by the Injection Molding Unit (Asset ID: 2847.420.0023) and the Horizontal CNC Machining Center (Asset ID: 2847.310.0042). It encompasses all products requiring inspection as part of the quality assurance process.

## 3. Prerequisites
Personnel performing visual inspections shall have completed the necessary training on quality standards and inspection techniques. They must be familiar with the AIQS, specifically clauses AIQS 4.5.3 and AIQS 4.5.2, which outline the requirements for inspection processes and defect categorization. Additionally, all inspectors shall have participated in an Operational Readiness Review (ORR) and be equipped with the appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) as per AIQS 4.2.4. Prior to inspection, ensure that the equipment is in a safe operating condition and that Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedures are followed where applicable.

## 4. Procedure
1. **Preparation**: Ensure the inspection area is clean and well-lit. Gather necessary tools, including magnifying glasses and inspection templates.
2. **Initial Inspection**: Begin by visually examining the component for any obvious surface defects such as scratches, dents, or discoloration. Refer to the defect classification guide per AIQS 4.5.3.
3. **Detailed Inspection**: Use a magnifying glass to closely inspect critical areas, focusing on edges and corners where defects are more likely to occur. Document any findings that deviate from the acceptable standards outlined in AIQS 4.5.2.
4. **Defect Classification**: Classify any identified defects according to severity levels: minor, major, or critical. Record the findings in the inspection log and prepare a Nonconformance Report (NCR) for any major or critical defects as per AIQS 3.4.3.
5. **Final Review**: After completing the inspection, review all findings with a supervisor. Ensure that all documentation is complete and that any necessary corrective actions are initiated before the component proceeds to the next stage of production.

## 5. Verification
Verification of the inspection process shall be conducted through periodic audits as outlined in AIQS 4.1.4. Inspectors shall maintain records of inspections for traceability and accountability. Supervisors shall review inspection logs weekly to ensure compliance with established criteria and to identify any trends in defects. Any discrepancies shall be addressed immediately to uphold quality standards.
