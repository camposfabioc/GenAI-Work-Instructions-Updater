# WI-017: Corrective Action Request Processing

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Work Instruction (WI) is to outline the process for managing Corrective Action Requests (CARs) in accordance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS). This ensures that all nonconformances are identified, documented, and resolved effectively, thereby maintaining product quality and compliance per AIQS 1.1.1.

## 2. Scope
This WI applies to all personnel involved in the identification, documentation, and resolution of CARs within the manufacturing plant. It encompasses all processes and equipment, including the Injection Molding Unit (2847.420.0023) and the Coordinate Measuring Machine (2847.510.0007), as required by AIQS 2.5.3.

## 3. Prerequisites
Before initiating the CAR process, personnel must complete the Operational Readiness Review (ORR) and be familiar with the AIQS guidelines, particularly AIQS 1.2.1 and AIQS 1.2.2. Personnel must also have received training on the use of the equipment involved, including the Injection Molding Unit (2847.420.0023) and the Coordinate Measuring Machine (2847.510.0007). Additionally, personnel must understand the Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedures to ensure safety during equipment maintenance and inspections as per AIQS 2.2.3.

## 4. Procedure Step 1
Identify a nonconformance during production or inspection. Upon identification, complete a Nonconformance Report (NCR) detailing the nature of the issue, the affected equipment (e.g., 2847.420.0023), and any immediate corrective actions taken. Ensure that the NCR is submitted to the Quality Assurance (QA) department within 24 hours of detection, as mandated by AIQS 2.3.1. The QA department shall review the NCR for completeness and accuracy, ensuring that all relevant information is captured to facilitate further investigation.

## 5. Procedure Step 2
Once the NCR is approved, the QA department shall assign a CAR to the responsible department. The assigned personnel must conduct a root cause analysis to determine the underlying cause of the nonconformance. This analysis should involve reviewing production records, equipment logs, and any relevant documentation. The findings must be documented in the CAR form, referencing the specific AIQS clauses related to the issue, particularly AIQS 2.3.2, which outlines the requirements for corrective actions.

## 6. Procedure Step 3
Develop a corrective action plan based on the root cause analysis. The plan must include specific actions to address the identified issues, responsible personnel, and timelines for implementation. Ensure that the plan aligns with the corrective action requirements outlined in AIQS 2.5.1. The corrective action plan shall be reviewed and approved by the QA department before implementation. Once approved, communicate the plan to all relevant personnel and ensure they understand their roles in executing the corrective actions.

## 7. Procedure Step 4
Implement the corrective action plan and monitor its effectiveness. Personnel responsible for executing the plan must document the actions taken and any changes made to processes or equipment. After implementation, conduct a follow-up review to assess whether the corrective actions have resolved the nonconformance. This review must be documented and submitted to the QA department for final approval, ensuring compliance with AIQS 2.5.3 and confirming that the issue has been effectively addressed.

## 8. Verification
To verify the effectiveness of the corrective actions, the QA department shall conduct an audit of the processes and equipment involved. This audit should include a review of the CAR documentation, implementation of corrective actions, and any changes made to prevent recurrence. The results of the audit must be documented, and any further actions required should be initiated promptly, in accordance with AIQS 2.3.1.

## 9. Records
All records related to the CAR process, including NCRs, CAR forms, root cause analyses, corrective action plans, and audit results, shall be maintained in accordance with AIQS 1.1.1. These records must be accessible for review and must be retained for a minimum of three years to ensure compliance with quality standards and facilitate continuous improvement initiatives.
