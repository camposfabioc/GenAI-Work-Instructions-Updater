# WI-005: Root Cause Analysis Using 5-Why Method

## 1. Purpose  
This Work Instruction (WI) outlines the process for conducting a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) using the 5-Why method. The objective is to identify the underlying causes of nonconformities to prevent recurrence, in compliance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS) requirements, specifically per AIQS 5.4.1.

## 2. Scope  
This WI applies to all personnel involved in quality assurance and problem-solving activities within the manufacturing plant. It encompasses all equipment, including the Coordinate Measuring Machine (Asset ID: 2847.510.0007) and the Hydraulic Press, 12-ton (Asset ID: 2847.420.0018), and aligns with AIQS 5.4.4 and AIQS 4.2.2.

## 3. Procedure  
1. **Identify the Problem**: Document the issue that has occurred and ensure it is logged in the Nonconformance Report (NCR) system. Refer to AIQS 5.4.1 for documentation requirements.

2. **Assemble the Team**: Gather a cross-functional team with knowledge of the process and equipment involved. Ensure that team members understand the importance of the RCA process as outlined in AIQS 5.4.4.

3. **Conduct the 5-Why Analysis**:  
   a. Start with the identified problem and ask "Why?" to determine the first cause.  
   b. Record the answer and ask "Why?" again for each subsequent answer until you reach the root cause, typically after five iterations.  
   c. Document each step clearly, ensuring compliance with AIQS 4.2.2 regarding record-keeping.

4. **Develop Corrective Actions**: Once the root cause is identified, brainstorm corrective actions to eliminate the cause. Ensure that these actions are feasible and do not introduce new risks.

5. **Implement and Verify**: Execute the corrective actions and monitor their effectiveness. Conduct a follow-up to confirm that the issue has been resolved and that the corrective actions are sustainable.

6. **Review and Close**: Update the NCR with the findings and actions taken. Ensure that the RCA process is reviewed during the Operational Readiness Review (ORR) to prevent future occurrences.
