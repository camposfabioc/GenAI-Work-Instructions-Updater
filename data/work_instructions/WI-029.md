# WI-029: Equipment Decommissioning Checklist

## 1. Purpose
This Work Instruction (WI) outlines the standardized process for decommissioning equipment, specifically the Coordinate Measuring Machine (CMM) 2847.510.0007 and the Hydraulic Press, 12-ton 2847.420.0018. The purpose is to ensure compliance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS) and to maintain safety and quality during the decommissioning process per AIQS 3.5.2.

## 2. Scope
This WI applies to all personnel involved in the decommissioning of equipment within the manufacturing facility. It encompasses the necessary steps to safely and effectively remove equipment from service while adhering to AIQS standards, particularly AIQS 2.4.3.

## 3. Prerequisites
Before commencing the decommissioning process, personnel shall complete the following prerequisites: 
1. Conduct an Operational Readiness Review (ORR) to ensure all safety measures are in place per AIQS 2.2.1.
2. Review the equipment's maintenance history and ensure that any outstanding Nonconformance Reports (NCRs) are resolved as per AIQS 2.3.3.
3. Ensure that all personnel involved in the decommissioning process are trained in Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedures and familiar with the equipment specifications.
4. Obtain necessary approvals from the Quality Assurance (QA) department and document any required First Article Inspections (FAIs) as per AIQS 3.4.3.

## 4. Procedure Step 1
Initiate the decommissioning process by notifying the relevant departments of the planned decommissioning of equipment 2847.510.0007 and 2847.420.0018. Schedule a meeting with the maintenance team to discuss the timeline and specific tasks involved. Ensure that all team members understand their roles and responsibilities. Conduct a thorough review of the equipment's operational status and document any issues that may affect the decommissioning process. Confirm that all safety protocols are in place and that the LOTO procedure is implemented before proceeding. This step must be completed in accordance with AIQS 2.3.4 to ensure a safe working environment.

## 5. Procedure Step 2
Proceed with the physical disconnection of the equipment from power sources. Implement the Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedure to ensure that the equipment is completely isolated from energy sources. Verify that all energy sources, including electrical, hydraulic, and pneumatic, are properly locked out and tagged. Conduct a thorough inspection of the equipment to identify any potential hazards or remaining connections that require attention. Document any findings and ensure that all safety measures are adhered to as per AIQS 3.5.2. Once all checks are complete, prepare the equipment for removal by coordinating with the logistics team to arrange for transportation to the designated decommissioning area.

## 6. Procedure Step 3
Once the equipment has been safely transported to the decommissioning area, conduct a final inspection to ensure that all components are accounted for and that no hazardous materials remain. Prepare a detailed report documenting the decommissioning process, including any issues encountered and resolutions applied. Submit the report to the QA department for review and approval, ensuring compliance with AIQS 2.4.3. Dispose of any non-reusable components in accordance with environmental regulations and company policies. Ensure that all records of the decommissioning process are maintained for future reference and audits, as required by AIQS 2.3.4.

## 7. Verification
After completing the decommissioning process, the QA department shall conduct a verification audit to ensure compliance with all steps outlined in this WI. This audit will include reviewing documentation, confirming that all safety protocols were followed, and ensuring that any NCRs were addressed. The audit results shall be documented and filed in accordance with AIQS 2.2.1. Any discrepancies identified during the verification process must be rectified immediately, and corrective actions shall be documented as per AIQS 3.4.3.

## 8. Records
All records related to the decommissioning of equipment 2847.510.0007 and 2847.420.0018 shall be maintained in a secure location for a minimum of five years. This includes the ORR documentation, LOTO checklists, inspection reports, and the final decommissioning report. Ensure that these records are readily accessible for audits and reviews as required by AIQS 2.3.3 and AIQS 2.4.3.
