# WI-016: Torque Wrench Calibration Verification

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Work Instruction (WI) is to outline the procedure for verifying the calibration of torque wrenches used in manufacturing processes. This ensures compliance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS) and maintains the integrity of the production process per AIQS 3.3.1.

## 2. Scope
This WI applies to all personnel involved in the calibration and verification of torque wrenches within the manufacturing facility. It covers equipment asset IDs 2847.310.0061 (Vertical Turning Lathe) and 2847.420.0018 (Hydraulic Press, 12-ton) as specified in AIQS 3.2.2.

## 3. Prerequisites
Before commencing the torque wrench calibration verification, personnel must complete the following prerequisites: 
1. Review the AIQS documentation, particularly clauses AIQS 3.1.1 and AIQS 3.1.4, to understand the quality standards related to calibration.
2. Ensure that the torque wrenches to be verified are properly identified and logged.
3. Conduct an Operational Readiness Review (ORR) to confirm that all necessary tools, including calibration standards, are available and in good condition.
4. Verify that the torque wrenches have not been subjected to any conditions that could affect their accuracy, such as physical damage or excessive wear, as per AIQS 3.4.4.

## 4. Procedure Step 1
Begin the verification process by gathering all necessary equipment, including the torque wrenches and calibration standards. Ensure that the calibration standards used are traceable to a national or international standard, in compliance with AIQS 3.3.3. Set up the Vertical Turning Lathe (2847.310.0061) in a controlled environment to minimize external influences on the calibration results. Perform a Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedure on the lathe to ensure safety during the calibration process. Document the setup conditions and any observations that may impact the calibration verification.

## 5. Procedure Step 2
Next, apply the calibration standard to the torque wrench. Gradually increase the torque setting on the wrench to the specified calibration points. Use a calibrated measuring device to verify the output torque at each point. Record the actual torque readings and compare them against the specified values. If discrepancies are noted, initiate a Nonconformance Report (NCR) as outlined in AIQS 2.4.4. If the torque wrench is found to be out of tolerance, it shall be removed from service until recalibrated. Ensure that all readings are documented accurately for traceability.

## 6. Procedure Step 3
After completing the verification of each torque wrench, perform a final inspection to confirm that all tools are returned to their designated storage locations. Update the calibration records to reflect the verification results, including any adjustments made or NCRs issued. Ensure that the Hydraulic Press (2847.420.0018) is also checked for any torque-related applications that may require verification. Follow the guidelines set forth in AIQS 3.5.4 to maintain the integrity of the calibration process and ensure compliance with quality standards.

## 7. Verification
Verification of the calibration process shall be conducted by a qualified individual who is independent of the calibration activity. This individual shall review all documentation, including calibration records and NCRs, to ensure compliance with AIQS 3.3.1. Any findings shall be documented, and corrective actions shall be implemented as necessary. The verification process shall also include a review of the calibration standards used to ensure they remain within their calibration intervals.

## 8. Records
All records related to the torque wrench calibration verification shall be maintained in accordance with AIQS 3.2.2. This includes calibration logs, NCRs, and verification reports. Records shall be stored in a secure location and made accessible for audit purposes. Ensure that records are retained for a minimum of three years or as specified by the AIQS guidelines.
