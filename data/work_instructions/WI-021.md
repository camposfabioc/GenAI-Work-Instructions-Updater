# WI-021: In-Process Dimensional Check Procedure

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Work Instruction (WI) is to outline the procedure for conducting in-process dimensional checks on manufactured components to ensure compliance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS). This process is critical for maintaining product quality and preventing defects during production.

## 2. Scope
This WI applies to all personnel involved in the manufacturing process of components using equipment asset IDs 2847.310.0055 (Precision Surface Grinder) and 2847.310.0042 (Horizontal CNC Machining Center). It encompasses all stages of production where dimensional accuracy is essential, as per AIQS requirements.

## 3. Prerequisites
Before commencing the in-process dimensional check, personnel shall complete the Operational Readiness Review (ORR) to ensure that all equipment is functioning correctly. Personnel must be trained in the use of measuring tools and familiar with the AIQS standards, specifically AIQS 4.5.1 and AIQS 4.1.3. Ensure that all necessary measuring instruments, such as calipers and micrometers, are calibrated and in good condition. Additionally, personnel must perform Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedures on the equipment to ensure safety during the measurement process. A Nonconformance Report (NCR) shall be prepared if any discrepancies are found during the checks.

## 4. Procedure Step 1
Initiate the in-process dimensional check by verifying that the equipment is set up according to the specifications outlined in the work order. For the Precision Surface Grinder (2847.310.0055), ensure that the grinding wheel is properly dressed and the coolant system is operational. For the Horizontal CNC Machining Center (2847.310.0042), confirm that the program is loaded and the tool offsets are correctly entered. Refer to AIQS 3.4.1 for specific setup requirements. Once the setup is confirmed, perform a preliminary check of the machine's operational parameters to ensure they meet the production specifications.

## 5. Procedure Step 2
After confirming the equipment setup, begin the dimensional check by selecting the appropriate measuring tools for the specific dimensions to be checked. Utilize calipers for external measurements and micrometers for internal measurements, ensuring that all tools are calibrated as per AIQS 3.2.1. Measure the first component at designated points as specified in the engineering drawings. Record all measurements accurately, noting any deviations from the specified tolerances. If any measurement falls outside the acceptable range, immediately halt production and escalate the issue as per AIQS 3.5.4.

## 6. Procedure Step 3
Continue the dimensional checks for subsequent components, following the same measurement protocol established in Procedure Step 2. Ensure that each component is measured at multiple points to verify consistency and accuracy. Document all findings in the designated quality control log. If discrepancies are identified, initiate a Nonconformance Report (NCR) per AIQS 3.3.3 and notify the quality assurance team for further investigation. Maintain a clear record of all measurements to facilitate traceability and compliance with AIQS 4.5.1.

## 7. Procedure Step 4
Upon completion of the dimensional checks for all components, compile the measurement data and analyze it for trends or recurring issues. If any components consistently fail to meet specifications, conduct a root cause analysis to determine the underlying issue. Adjust the manufacturing process as necessary to rectify any identified problems. Ensure that all findings are communicated to the production team and documented in the quality control report. Follow up with a First Article Inspection (FAI) if significant changes are made to the process, as per AIQS 4.1.3.

## 8. Verification
Verify the accuracy of the dimensional checks by cross-referencing the recorded measurements with the engineering specifications. Conduct a review of the quality control log to ensure all data is complete and accurate. If any discrepancies are found during verification, initiate corrective actions immediately and document the process. Ensure that all verification activities comply with AIQS 3.4.1 and that personnel involved in the verification process are qualified and trained.

## 9. Records
Maintain all records related to the in-process dimensional checks, including measurement logs, NCRs, and any corrective action documentation. These records shall be stored in accordance with AIQS 4.5.1 and be readily accessible for audits and reviews. Ensure that all records are retained for the required duration as specified in the quality management system. Regularly review and update records to maintain compliance with AIQS standards.
