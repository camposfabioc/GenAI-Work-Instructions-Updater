# WI-026: Corrective Action Implementation and Tracking

## 1. Purpose  
This Work Instruction (WI) outlines the process for implementing and tracking corrective actions in compliance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS). It aims to ensure nonconformances are addressed effectively to maintain product quality and operational efficiency per AIQS 5.1.1.

## 2. Scope  
This WI applies to all personnel involved in the corrective action process within the manufacturing plant. It encompasses the use of equipment asset IDs 2847.420.0018 (Hydraulic Press, 12-ton) and 2847.310.0061 (Vertical Turning Lathe) during corrective actions as stipulated in AIQS 4.4.3.

## 3. Procedure  
1. Identify the nonconformance and document it in a Nonconformance Report (NCR) per AIQS 4.2.2. Ensure that the NCR includes detailed descriptions of the issue, affected equipment (e.g., 2847.420.0018), and any relevant operational impacts.

2. Conduct an Operational Readiness Review (ORR) to assess the root cause of the nonconformance. Engage relevant personnel and utilize tools such as the 5 Whys or Fishbone Diagram to facilitate this analysis. Document findings in the NCR.

3. Develop a corrective action plan that includes specific actions, responsible personnel, and deadlines for completion. Ensure that the plan addresses the root cause identified during the ORR and complies with AIQS 4.2.4.

4. Implement the corrective actions while adhering to Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedures to ensure safety during equipment operation, particularly for the Vertical Turning Lathe (2847.310.0061).

5. Monitor the effectiveness of the corrective actions over a defined period. Review the outcomes against the expected results documented in the corrective action plan. Update the NCR with findings and close it out once the corrective actions are verified as effective per AIQS 4.4.3. 

6. Conduct a First Article Inspection (FAI) if applicable, to validate that the corrective actions have resolved the nonconformance and that the product meets quality standards.
