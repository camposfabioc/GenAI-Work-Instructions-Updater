# WI-007: Tool Wear Monitoring and Replacement

## 1. Purpose  
The purpose of this Work Instruction (WI) is to outline the process for monitoring and replacing worn tools in accordance with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS). Effective tool management ensures optimal performance of equipment and minimizes production downtime, thereby maintaining product quality as per AIQS 3.3.4.

## 2. Scope  
This WI applies to all personnel involved in the operation and maintenance of the Vertical Turning Lathe (2847.310.0061) and the Injection Molding Unit (2847.420.0023). It encompasses the monitoring of tool wear, the criteria for replacement, and documentation requirements as specified in AIQS 3.2.1.

## 3. Prerequisites  
Personnel must complete the Operational Readiness Review (ORR) before performing tool wear monitoring. Training on the Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedures is mandatory to ensure safety during tool replacement. Additionally, operators must be familiar with the Nonconformance Report (NCR) process and the First Article Inspection (FAI) requirements to document any deviations from expected tool performance, as outlined in AIQS 2.3.2.

## 4. Procedure  
1. **Monitoring Tool Wear**  
   a. Inspect tools at the beginning of each shift.  
   b. Use a calibrated gauge to measure tool dimensions and compare them against the specifications outlined in the equipment manual.  
   c. Document any wear exceeding acceptable limits in the maintenance log as per AIQS 2.4.1.  

2. **Assessing Tool Condition**  
   a. Evaluate the tool's performance during operation.  
   b. If the tool produces defects or inconsistent results, initiate an NCR.  
   c. Conduct a visual inspection for chips, cracks, or other damage.  

3. **Replacing Worn Tools**  
   a. Follow LOTO procedures to ensure the equipment is safely powered down.  
   b. Remove the worn tool using the appropriate tool removal techniques as specified in the equipment manual.  
   c. Install the new tool, ensuring it is properly secured and aligned.  
   d. Conduct a test run to verify tool performance and document results in the maintenance log.  

4. **Final Documentation**  
   a. Complete all necessary documentation, including tool wear measurements and replacement details.  
   b. Submit the documentation for review as per AIQS 3.2.1.  

## 5. Verification  
Verification of tool wear monitoring and replacement shall be conducted monthly by the Quality Assurance team. They shall review maintenance logs and NCRs to ensure compliance with AIQS 2.3.2 and AIQS 2.4.1. Any discrepancies shall be addressed immediately to maintain operational integrity and product quality.
