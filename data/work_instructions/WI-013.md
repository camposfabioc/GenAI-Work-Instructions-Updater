# WI-013: Employee Onboarding for Quality Systems

## 1. Purpose
The purpose of this Work Instruction (WI) is to outline the onboarding process for new employees regarding Quality Systems at Acme Industrial. This process ensures that all personnel are familiar with the Acme Industrial Quality Standard (AIQS) and are equipped to maintain compliance with quality requirements as specified in AIQS 1.3.2.

## 2. Scope
This WI applies to all new employees in the manufacturing plant who will be involved in quality-related tasks. It encompasses training on quality standards, equipment operation, and adherence to procedures as outlined in AIQS 2.3.3.

## 3. Prerequisites
Before commencing onboarding, the following prerequisites must be met: 
1. The employee must have completed the initial HR orientation and received their employee ID.
2. The employee shall have access to the Quality Management System (QMS) documentation.
3. The employee must be assigned a mentor from the Quality Assurance (QA) team.
4. The employee shall complete the Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) training and pass the associated assessment.
5. The employee must review the AIQS clauses relevant to their role, particularly AIQS 1.2.4 and AIQS 1.5.3, to understand the quality expectations.

## 4. Procedure Step 1
The onboarding process shall begin with an introductory meeting between the new employee and their assigned mentor. During this meeting, the mentor shall provide an overview of the AIQS, focusing on critical clauses such as AIQS 1.3.1 and AIQS 1.5.4. The mentor shall explain the importance of quality systems in manufacturing and how they impact overall operational efficiency. The employee shall be encouraged to ask questions and clarify any doubts regarding the AIQS. Additionally, the mentor shall outline the specific quality-related responsibilities of the employee's role, ensuring that the employee understands the significance of compliance with quality standards.

## 5. Procedure Step 2
Following the introductory meeting, the employee shall participate in hands-on training with key equipment, specifically the Hydraulic Press (Asset ID: 2847.420.0018) and the Vertical Turning Lathe (Asset ID: 2847.310.0061). The mentor shall demonstrate the proper operation of each machine and highlight the quality checks required per AIQS 1.4.1. The employee shall practice operating the equipment under supervision, ensuring they can perform First Article Inspections (FAI) and recognize potential Nonconformance Reports (NCR). The mentor shall assess the employee's competency in operating the equipment and performing quality checks, providing feedback and additional training as necessary.

## 6. Procedure Step 3
The final step in the onboarding process shall involve a formal Operational Readiness Review (ORR) conducted by the mentor and the Quality Manager. The employee shall present their understanding of the AIQS and demonstrate their ability to operate the assigned equipment while adhering to quality standards. The mentor shall evaluate the employee's performance against the criteria set forth in AIQS 2.3.3. Upon successful completion of the ORR, the employee shall receive a certification of completion for the onboarding process, confirming their readiness to contribute to quality assurance efforts within the plant.

## 7. Verification
Verification of the onboarding process shall be conducted through a series of assessments and observations. The mentor shall complete a checklist to confirm that all training components have been covered, including the employee's understanding of AIQS clauses and equipment operation. The Quality Manager shall review the completed checklist and the ORR results to ensure compliance with AIQS 1.3.2. Any discrepancies or areas for improvement shall be documented and addressed promptly.

## 8. Records
All records related to the onboarding process shall be maintained in the Quality Management System (QMS). This includes the completed onboarding checklist, ORR results, and any training certificates issued. Records shall be retained for a minimum of three years per AIQS 1.5.4, ensuring that all onboarding activities are traceable and compliant with quality standards.
